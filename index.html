Cardamom

Cardamom, renowned as the "Queen of Spices," traces its origins to the lush Western Ghats of southern India, thriving for over 4,000 years and earning admiration from ancient civilizations.

Cloves

Cloves, originating from the dried flower buds of the Syzygium aromaticum tree, have their roots in the Indonesian Maluku Islands and were pivotal in the historic spice trade along the Silk Road.

Mace

Derived from the aril of the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrant). Mace possesses a unique history and application. Both Mace and nutmeg originate from Indonesia's Spice Islands and gained prominence through Arab traders in the ancient spice trade routes.

Black pepper

Black pepper, originating from the Piper nigrum vine in India's Western Ghats, has been prized for over 4,000 years. Its role in the historic spice trade, connecting India to the Roman Empire and influencing European explorers, holds cultural and economic significance

Red Chilli Flakes.

Red chilli flakes, typically sourced from dried and crushed red chilli peppers like cayenne and bird's eye chilli, have their origins in the Americas, where chilli peppers have been cultivated for millennia.

Green Chilli Flakes.

Green chilli flakes, derived from dried and crushed green chillies, have a vibrant history, originating from the diverse culinary landscapes of regions like Mexico, Southeast Asia, and India.
